v1.3.1 Universal Gateway

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 20, 2026

Nexus Protocol is a Local-First Sovereign Gateway. We do not operate centralized servers to collect or store your data. Privacy is enforced through local execution and cryptographic verification.

1. Universal Identity Handling

Nexus handles identity metadata exclusively at the edge. The architecture is designed to support multiple ecosystems (e.g., peaq, IoTeX, TON, Solana) via adapter interfaces.

  • Machine Identity (peaq/IoTeX): Device identifiers (peaq ID, ioID) are stored locally in your nexus_vault.db. This data verifies hardware integrity but is never transmitted to Nexus Protocol developers.
  • User Identity (Social/Web3): Request signatures (HMAC, Ed25519) are parsed locally to validate intents. No session data is uploaded to cloud servers.

2. Off-Chain Compute & Anchoring

Nexus utilizes a "Verify-then-Execute" pattern. Data proofs (e.g., for W3bstream) or State Roots (for L1 Anchoring) are generated locally. You explicitly control when and where these proofs are published to a public blockchain.

3. Data Custody

You are the sole custodian of your node's database file. Nexus does not perform behavioral analytics, centralized profiling, or cloud-sync operations. Nexus Protocol does not transmit data to any Nexus-operated servers, as no such servers exist in Phase 1.3.1. Your data availability depends entirely on the integrity of your own hardware.
