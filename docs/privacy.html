Sovereign Phase 1.3

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 17, 2026

Nexus Protocol operates as a Local-First Sovereign Node. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal data on any central server. Our architecture is designed to enforce privacy through local execution.

1. Local-First Sovereignty

In Phase 1.3, all economic state transitions, ledger entries, and identifiers are processed by your local Sovereign Brain and stored exclusively in the nexus_vault.db file on your local machine. No data is shared with the Nexus developers or any third-party analytics provider.

2. Perimeter Security (The Sentry)

Phase 1.3 introduces the Sentry, a request-legitimacy layer. The Sentry parses Telegram initData signatures to verify that incoming requests are legitimate. This process happens entirely on your local node. We do not store or transmit these Telegram signatures to external servers.

3. Stateless Body (UI)

The Nexus Body remains a stateless visualization layer. It carries the X-Nexus-TMA integrity payload required to interact with your local Brain but does not persist financial data or ledger history in your browser’s local storage.

4. Third-Party Network Bridges

If you utilize external bridges (e.g., Ngrok) to access your node via the Telegram Mini App interface, your data is encrypted in transit. Nexus Protocol does not control these bridges and encourages users to review the privacy policies of any tunneling service they employ.

5. Cryptographic Ownership

Nexus Protocol does not currently utilize private keys or blockchain-based identity. Personal data interaction is limited to parsing display-only metadata provided by the Telegram API for local rendering purposes.
