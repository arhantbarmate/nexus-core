Technical FAQ

Sovereign Intel · Infrastructure Logic
Why use a custom HTML proxy for Ngrok?
To maintain $0-cost Infrastructure Sovereignty. The Ngrok free tier displays an interstitial warning that breaks headless handshakes. Our Sentry Bridge bypasses this via automated header logic and a client-side skip-ui to ensure seamless handshakes during development and demos.
How is concurrency handled in local hardware?
We utilize SQLite Write-Ahead Logging (WAL). This allows the Nexus Brain to handle high-frequency 60/30/10 split settlements without database locking, even when hit by a **50-user concurrent identity surge**.
Why frame the UI as an "Execution Surface"?
To maintain Chain-Agnosticism. The UI merely renders the state of the Sovereign Brain. This separation ensures Nexus remains compatible with future machine, device, or DID-based identity systems (like peaq or IoTeX) without altering core execution logic.
